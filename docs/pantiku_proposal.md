## PROBLEM DEFINITION

### Main Problem

The main problem faced by orphanages in Indonesia is not just limited funding but the absence of a sustainable care and empowerment system. Management patterns dependent on one-way donations cause assistance to often not match the real needs of orphanages and children. Children grow up in an environment focused on meeting basic needs, but with minimal emotional support, potential development, and future readiness. In the long term, this condition creates dependency, low self-esteem, and limited life skills. On the orphanage side, limited access to productive business models and marketing makes it difficult for orphanages to be economically independent and continue to depend on external aid.

### Who is Affected and How Much?

The directly affected parties are children living in orphanages, particularly in psychosocial aspects, personal development, and readiness for independent living. Orphanage managers are affected by resource limitations and sustainable management models. In the long term, orphanages have the potential to transform into communities producing superior talent through structured care, skills, and mentoring programs. This opens opportunities for the birth of a skilled workforce from orphanages, so businesses and industry can see orphanages as part of the national talent development ecosystem.

### Evidence of the Problem

Based on field observations and common practices, the distribution of assistance to orphanages is uneven and seasonal. During certain periods such as religious holidays, orphanages can receive assistance exceeding capacity, while on other days they receive almost no assistance at all, even though needs are constant and tend to increase. Surges in assistance often overwhelm managers in management, and they are even forced to resell received assistance, risking misuse. Additionally, donors tend to help orphanages that are easily accessible geographically, while orphanages in remote areas struggle to get assistance. The barrier of costs and minimum visit amounts also limits participation from small donors.

## PROPOSED SOLUTION

### Core Solution

Pantiku is a digital platform that builds a comprehensive care and empowerment ecosystem for orphanages. This solution integrates child and orphanage needs mapping, emotional support and skills development, as well as orphanage economic empowerment through productive activities. Pantiku transforms the concept of one-way donations into sustained collaboration by facilitating campaigns for productive tools and capital, management mentoring, and marketing of orphanage production through the marketplace. Pantiku also facilitates a foster parent scheme, where married couples can fully support a child's living expenses and education without separating the child from the orphanage environment, so the child grows up stably until ready to continue higher education. With this approach, orphanage children not only grow with their needs met but also possess confidence, skills, and readiness for independent living.

### How the Solution Works

**Input:** Child and orphanage needs data, donor participation, volunteers, and partners.

**Process:** Pantiku maps needs, facilitates campaigns for productive facilities, emotional support and skills development, and manages orphanage productive activities. Child development is carried out efficiently by grouping children from several orphanages in one area based on interests and talents to participate in regular joint training, improving the quality of mentoring at a more affordable cost. Orphanage production is marketed through the Pantiku marketplace with a donation-based purchase mechanism. Pantiku groups children from several orphanages in one area based on interests and talents to participate in regular training together, so trainer and facility costs can be reduced without compromising the quality of mentoring.

**Output:** Orphanages obtain sustainable income sources, children receive comprehensive care and life skills, and donors participate in a transparent, inclusive, and long-term impactful social-economic ecosystem.

## INNOVATION & DIFFERENTIATION

### What Makes This Solution Different?

Pantiku presents innovation by combining emotional care, skills development, and orphanage economic empowerment in one integrated digital platform. Unlike conventional donation platforms that focus on fundraising, Pantiku transforms the donation paradigm into productive collaboration through campaigns for business tools and marketing their products on the marketplace. Pantiku also provides space for donor communities and forums to network and collaborate, so involvement doesn't stop at donations but develops into sustainable social and professional relationships. This approach builds orphanage independence and child future readiness sustainably.

### Solution Position Compared to Existing Approaches or Products

Existing approaches typically position orphanages as passive aid recipients and donors as one-way fund providers. Pantiku goes beyond this approach by creating a two-way ecosystem connecting orphanages, donors, volunteers, and digital markets. This solution complements existing donation platforms by introducing layers of economic empowerment and structured child mentoring, so the resulting impact is more sustainable and measurable in the long term.

## TECHNICAL APPROACH

### Main Technology Used

Pantiku is developed as a digital platform based on web and mobile applications to connect orphanages, children, and contributors in one ecosystem. Main technologies include mobile applications, centralized data management systems, orphanage and program management dashboards, and integrated marketplace features. The platform is designed to support needs mapping, program implementation, donation-based transactions, and transparent impact reporting.

### Technology Selection and Use

Web and mobile technologies are chosen to make the platform easily accessible by orphanages and donors across various regions. Data management systems are used to map child and orphanage needs and monitor program progress. Marketplace features allow for simple and inclusive transactions, while dashboards are used by orphanage managers and Pantiku teams for management, monitoring, and ongoing program accountability.

### Solution Algorithm

Pantiku uses a rule-based matching approach to match orphanage and child needs with available types of contributions. This algorithm groups data based on need categories, orphanage capacity, and contributor preferences. This mechanism ensures assistance, programs, and productive activities are better targeted. At a later stage, the system can be developed using data analysis to improve the accuracy of program and contribution recommendations.

### Main Data and Inputs Used

Main data used includes orphanage profiles, aggregated child data, types of needs, productive activities, and contribution and transaction data. Data is obtained from input by orphanage managers, volunteers, and donors through the platform and verified by the Pantiku team. This approach ensures data is relevant, current, and can be used as a basis for program decision-making and impact reporting.

### Security and Scalability Considerations

Pantiku implements data security principles with role-based access restrictions, sensitive data protection, and system activity logging. The platform architecture is designed modularly for easy development and scaling to more orphanages, users, and features in the future. This approach enables Pantiku to grow nationally without compromising system stability and security.

## IMPLEMENTATION FEASIBILITY

### Innovation Status

Currently, Pantiku is at the stage of structured concept and initial solution design. The team has developed the ecosystem model, user flows, and approaches for child care and orphanage empowerment programs. The next step is developing mockups and initial prototypes to be tested at pilot orphanages.

### Is the Innovation Realistic to Build?

Pantiku innovation is realistic to build because it uses commonly available and accessible technologies, such as web and mobile platforms. The team has a background in management, community management, and operational technology relevant to running the initial stages of innovation. The solution model is developed gradually, starting with core features like needs mapping, productive campaigns, and simple marketplace, allowing measured and low-risk implementation in the early phase.

### Development Stages

The first stage begins with validating orphanage needs and developing platform design mockups. The second stage is prototype development with core features, including orphanage profiles, productive campaigns, and basic marketplace. The third stage conducts a pilot project at selected orphanages to test operational flows, donor participation, and productive activities. The next stage is evaluation, feature refinement, and preparation for wider-scale development based on pilot results and user feedback.

### Business Model and Sustainability

Pantiku's business model is designed as a sustainable social platform with an ecosystem approach. User segments include orphanages, orphanage children, individual donors, volunteers, and corporate partners. Pantiku's value proposition is comprehensive care and integrated digital orphanage economic empowerment. Revenue sources come from marketplace transaction service fees, corporate CSR program cooperation, and support from strategic partners. Main activities include platform management, program mentoring, and facilitation of orphanage product marketing. Key resources are digital platforms, orphanage needs data, and community networks. Cost structures include technology development, platform operations, and mentoring. This model ensures financial sustainability while maintaining focus on long-term social impact. Donor community involvement also opens opportunities for long-term collaboration and strategic partnerships within the Pantiku ecosystem.
